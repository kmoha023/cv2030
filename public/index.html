Here is what we have achieved so far

Looked at several sites ranging from 20 acres to over 100 acres west and south of our Masjid.

Have an Architect develop a conceptual plan for a 30 and 50-acre site which will house Masjid, Community Center/Soccer field/Basketball Courts/Single family and multi-family housing/Daycare & Adult Care/Assisted Living/Retail and many more amenities to call this place-Community Village.

Have discussed with members of Foundation Board to establish an autonomous group solely for the purpose of making Vision 2030 a reality.

Have presented the concept to 20 senior members of our Ummah, a small group at General Body Meeting and an Event attended by 145 guests. The reaction has been positive from all groups of people.

Vision 2030

Community Vision 2030 is a group of like-minded people who collectively saw the tremendous growth of Muslim ummah which, InshaAllah, will continue. This growth is presenting challenges upon our facilities which will keep on intensifying every year.

he transformation of our community’s activities from West Pine to the heart of West County was a vision by a group of pioneers. Our Masjid was the Pitney Bowes Building on SLU campus which served our needs for many years. With the Blessings of Allah SBWT and the foresight of our senior members moved here in this beautiful Masjid at Dar-ul-Salam while maintaining Masjid Bilal.

Many of the new-comers to this Masjid don’t appreciate the hard work and commitment of our founding members to provide us with this beautiful Masjid. Now, the challenges of the next phase are upon us to take lead. We are blessed us with this beautiful House of Allah and MashaAllah, we exceed the capacity of our Masjid on Fridays, Ramadan and Special Events. Parking and exiting out is time consuming, dangerous and challenging.

.

Problems we face and the solutions

The Shura is doing it’s best to manage our facilities which on many occasions exceed the intended occupancy posing a safety and security concern. The lack of meeting rooms and auditorium is direly missed. On Mondays, after Isha prayers, almost 300 people come to listen to Seerah program. The Tafsir room can’t accommodate 300 people, so they have to sit on the floor for over an hour. Imagine an Auditorium where we have comfortable seating, excellent acoustics and state of the art presentation devices. Why can’t we have this? .

Originally this group was organized in Fall of 2018 under Concerned Muslim Association and switched to STL Think Tank. When it was decided that the Group will work in collaboration with Islamic Foundation the name was changed to Community Vision 2030. The Mission and Goal of this organization, since it’s inception, remained unchanged--to look at our current situation of overcrowding at our Dar-ul-Salam facilities and explore solutions.
